## Typographic Directions

<div class="ref-grid">

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Swiss / International Style</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Typography</span><span class="ref-tag tag-era">1950s–</span><span class="ref-tag">Grid</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Helvetica, tight grids, flush-left text, no ornament. The aesthetic backbone of most serious editorial and corporate design. Key principle: type as structure, not decoration. Josef Müller-Brockmann's <em>Grid Systems</em> is the primary text.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://www.hgsd.edu/">HGSD reference</a> · <a href="https://designobserver.com/feature/the-grid/38059">Essay</a></div>
</div>

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Kinetic Typography</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Motion</span><span class="ref-tag tag-tool">GSAP</span><span class="ref-tag">CSS</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Text as choreography. Pentagram's digital work and Sagmeister & Walsh campaigns are key references. On the web: scroll-driven text reveals, character-by-character animations. Key tool: GSAP SplitText + ScrollTrigger.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://gsap.com/docs/v3/Plugins/SplitText/">GSAP SplitText</a> · <a href="https://pentagram.com/">Pentagram</a></div>
</div>

</div>

## Visual Aesthetics

<div class="ref-grid">

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Neo-Brutalism</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Visual</span><span class="ref-tag tag-era">2020s</span><span class="ref-tag">Web</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Bold borders, flat color fills, visible box-shadows with no blur, stark contrast. A reaction against the over-polished glassmorphism trend. Gumroad and Linear (early) are canonical web examples. Signals rawness and directness.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://hype4.academy/articles/design/neobrutalism-is-taking-over-web">Essay</a> · <a href="https://gumroad.com/">Gumroad (example)</a></div>
</div>

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Y2K / Poolsuite Aesthetic</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Visual</span><span class="ref-tag tag-era">1998–2004, 2020s revival</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Chrome gradients, bubbly type, translucent UI panels, cursor trails. Poolsuite FM is the best current web implementation — full OS-chrome metaphor with a vacation radio premise. The revival recontextualizes internet optimism with contemporary irony.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://poolsuite.net/">Poolsuite FM</a></div>
</div>

</div>

## Interaction Patterns

<div class="ref-grid">

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Scrollytelling</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Interaction</span><span class="ref-tag">Narrative</span><span class="ref-tag">Data Vis</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Scroll-position drives narrative and animation. Apple product pages (M-series chips) are the most polished commercial examples. For editorial: NYT Upshot and The Pudding. The core technique: Intersection Observer + CSS transform timelines.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://pudding.cool/">The Pudding</a> · <a href="https://apple.com/mac-pro/">Apple (example)</a></div>
</div>

<div class="ref-card">
<h3>Generative / p5.js</h3>
<span class="ref-tag tag-type">Generative</span><span class="ref-tag tag-tool">p5.js</span><span class="ref-tag">Creative Coding</span>
<p class="ref-desc">Code as a medium for visual art. p5.js makes Processing-style sketches browser-native. Key practitioners: Tyler Hobbs (flow fields), Vera Molnár (grid permutations). The OpenProcessing community is the best discovery feed.</p>
<div class="ref-links"><a href="https://p5js.org/">p5js.org</a> · <a href="https://openprocessing.org/">OpenProcessing</a> · <a href="https://www.tylerxhobbs.com/">Tyler Hobbs</a></div>
</div>

</div>

## Also Bookmarked

- **[Codrops](https://tympanus.net/codrops/)** — CSS/JS technique tutorials with live demos. Best source for WebGL particle effects and custom cursor implementations.
- **[CSS-Tricks / Almanac](https://css-tricks.com/almanac/)** — Layout pattern reference. Essential for grid + flexbox edge cases.
- **[Refactoring UI](https://www.refactoringui.com/)** — Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger. Practical visual design rules without theory-first overhead.
- **[Laws of UX](https://lawsofux.com/)** — Concise visual summaries of Fitts' Law, Hick's Law, Jakob's Law, etc. Good for quick reference before interface decisions.
- **[Fonts In Use](https://fontsinuse.com/)** — Reverse-lookup real-world typographic applications. Great for "what font does X use" research.
