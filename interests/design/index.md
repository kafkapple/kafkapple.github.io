## AI Agent Design

<div class="interest-item" style="border-left-color: rgb(46,85,56);">
<p class="interest-desc">Designing for LLM-powered agents demands a new interaction vocabulary — probabilistic outputs, non-deterministic state, and latent uncertainty must be treated explicitly in the interface. Three shifts define the challenge: <strong>managing non-determinism</strong> (the system can be wrong in unpredictable ways); <strong>designing for latency</strong> (token streaming is an interaction paradigm, not a loading state); <strong>calibrating agency</strong> (how much autonomy to delegate, and when to require human confirmation). This connects directly to questions of mental models and context-dependent cognition studied in NeuroAI — how users form a <em>theory of mind</em> for an AI agent is the critical factor for successful collaboration.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://pair.withgoogle.com/guidebook/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Google PAIR Guidebook</a> <span class="interest-tag">Framework</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Research-backed human-AI interaction patterns from Google's People + AI Research team. Covers mental models, error states, feedback loops, and expectation calibration across the full product lifecycle.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/haxtoolkit/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Microsoft HAX Toolkit</a> <span class="interest-tag">Framework</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">18 design guidelines distilled from Amershi et al. (CHI 2019) into actionable evaluation cards covering initial interaction, error recovery, and context change. The most practice-ready of the major AI design frameworks.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/machine-learning" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Apple ML Human Interface Guidelines</a> <span class="interest-tag">Platform</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Platform-specific guidance for ML integration: implicit vs. explicit intelligence, calibrated confidence display, and graceful degradation when model outputs are uncertain.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://www.ibm.com/design/ai/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">IBM Design for AI</a> <span class="interest-tag">Enterprise</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Enterprise framework organized around four properties — purposeful, responsible, transparent, human-centered — with specific guidance on conversational interfaces, automation disclosure, and explainability.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://www.nngroup.com/topic/artificial-intelligence/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nielsen Norman Group: AI & UX Research</a> <span class="interest-tag">Empirical</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Empirical UX research on LLM chat interfaces, AI writing tools, and agent workflows grounded in usability testing — particularly valuable for streaming text interaction patterns and trust calibration.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://hcil.umd.edu/human-centered-ai/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Human-Centered AI — Ben Shneiderman</a> <span class="interest-tag">Academic</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">The academic counterweight to AI-first narratives: tools that amplify human agency rather than replace human judgment. Essential framing for researchers coming from cognitive science or neuroscience.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title"><a href="https://www.anthropic.com/model-spec" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Anthropic Model Spec</a> <span class="interest-tag">Foundation</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Primarily an alignment document, but essential for interface designers: defines how Claude reasons about helpfulness, uncertainty, and refusal — directly informing confidence display, disclosure, and error-recovery UI patterns.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Product exemplars: <a href="https://www.perplexity.ai/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Perplexity</a> · <a href="https://v0.dev/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">v0.dev</a> · <a href="https://cursor.sh/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cursor</a> <span class="interest-tag">Live reference</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Three products demonstrating distinct principles: Perplexity solves the uncertainty problem via numbered citations (sourced synthesis); v0.dev externalizes reasoning via preview+code (process visibility); Cursor embeds AI as ambient context-aware pair programmer rather than a separate chat window (attentional framing).</p>
</div>

**Four principles for agent interface design:**
- **Expose process, not just product** — Render reasoning visibly: streaming traces, tool-use indicators, collapsible logs. Opacity in a probabilistic system erodes trust faster than any individual error.
- **Uncertainty is a first-class UI element** — Treat hedging, source citation, and explicit "I'm not sure" states as intentional information architecture, not failure modes to soften.
- **Error recovery must restore agency** — Offer concrete re-entry: editable context, alternative phrasings, scope controls. An apology with no affordance collapses the collaboration.
- **Calibrate autonomy to reversibility** — Autonomy granted should scale with reversibility: summarizing = minimal friction; writing to a database or sending a message = explicit gates and audit trail.
