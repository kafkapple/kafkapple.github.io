## Typographic Directions

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Swiss / International Style <span class="interest-tag">1950s–</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Helvetica, tight grids, flush-left text, no ornament. The aesthetic backbone of serious editorial and corporate design. Key text: Josef Müller-Brockmann's <em>Grid Systems</em>.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Kinetic Typography <span class="interest-tag">Motion</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Text as choreography. Pentagram's digital work and Sagmeister & Walsh campaigns. On the web: scroll-driven text reveals, character-by-character animations via GSAP SplitText.</p>
</div>

---

## Visual Aesthetics

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Neo-Brutalism <span class="interest-tag">2020s</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Bold borders, flat color fills, visible box-shadows with no blur, stark contrast. A reaction against glassmorphism. Gumroad and Linear (early) are canonical web examples.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Y2K / Poolsuite Aesthetic <span class="interest-tag">2020s revival</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Chrome gradients, bubbly type, translucent UI. Poolsuite FM is the best current implementation — full OS-chrome metaphor with vacation radio premise.</p>
</div>

---

## Interaction Patterns

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Scrollytelling <span class="interest-tag">Narrative</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Scroll-position drives narrative and animation. Apple product pages (M-series) are the most polished commercial examples. For editorial: NYT Upshot and The Pudding.</p>
</div>

<div class="interest-item">
<p class="interest-title">Generative / p5.js <span class="interest-tag">Creative Coding</span></p>
<p class="interest-desc">Code as visual art. Key practitioners: Tyler Hobbs (flow fields), Vera Molnár (grid permutations). The OpenProcessing community is the best discovery feed.</p>
</div>
